Vision: cCauvery

The vision seeks to preserve, restore, and sustain the ecological integrity of the Cauvery River Basin, recognizing its spiritual, cultural, and economic significance. This vision is built on four fundamental principles:

Fundamental Principles

  • Aviral Dhara (Uninterrupted Flow): Ensuring continuous and adequate water, sediment, and nutrient flow across the entire river system by regulating barriers, diversions, and surface runoff.
  • Nirmal Dhara (Unpolluted Flow): Restoring water quality by eliminating pollution from industrial, agricultural, and urban sources, promoting sustainable waste management, and maintaining ecological balance.
  • Geological Integrity: Protecting the river’s natural geological formations to prevent irreversible damage from unsustainable human interventions such as excessive groundwater extraction and land degradation.
  • Ecological Balance: Maintaining the river’s biodiversity by safeguarding aquatic life, preventing overexploitation, and promoting conservation practices that support long-term sustainability.

Implementation Strategy

To achieve this vision, scientific research, policy interventions, and community engagement are prioritized, integrating modern technology with traditional wisdom to ensure responsible water management.

Key Focus Areas

The Cauvery River Basin Management Plan focuses on:

  • Sustainable Agriculture
  • Flood Mitigation
  • Urban River Conservation
  • Disaster Preparedness
  • Water Governance

By implementing these strategies, we aim to ensure that the Cauvery River remains a lifeline for future generations.
